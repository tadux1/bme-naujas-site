Pneumatic systems

Pneumatic Systems Installation & Repair

Marine pneumatic systems installation and repair for marine and industrial equipment — we build, service and restore the valves, air piping, actuators and control air lines that operate remote valves, dampers, whistles and automation, on board or in our workshop.

Overview

Compressed air systems restored to reliable working pressure

A pneumatic fault is often quiet at first — a valve that actuates slowly, a pressure that will not hold, air escaping somewhere along the line. Left unaddressed, it turns into remote valves that will not close, controls that drift and automation that trips. Because compressed air feeds so many systems at once, a single leak or blockage can affect operations well beyond its source.

Our engineers work through the whole circuit — air preparation, piping, control valves and actuators — rather than replacing the first suspect part. We trace pressure drops, test for leaks under working conditions and confirm each actuator strokes and seats correctly. New installations are routed, supported and pressure-tested to hold, so the system stays clean, dry and dependable in service.

Reliable air also depends on its source and its preparation. Where the compressor, dryer or air quality is part of the problem, we address it as part of the job or alongside our compressor repair, so the pneumatic system is restored end to end rather than patched at one point.

Often delivered alongside marine compressor repair, hydraulic systems repair, marine engine repair.

Scope of work

What pneumatic systems installation and repair includes

  • Pneumatic valve repair — overhaul of directional, control and safety valves, reseating, seal replacement and function testing
  • Air piping installation — routing, fitting, supporting and pressure-testing of compressed air lines and connections
  • Pneumatic actuator service — cylinders and rotary actuators, seal kits, stroke setting and end-position adjustment
  • Leak detection and testing — locating air losses across the system, pressure hold and flow checks under working conditions
  • Control air systems — instrument and control air, filters, regulators and lubricators (FRL) service and setting
  • Air preparation — dryers, filters, water separators and condensate drains to keep the supply clean and dry
  • Fault diagnosis — tracing pressure drops, sticking valves and slow actuation through the circuit to the root cause
  • Commissioning and setting — start-up, calibration and functional testing of the completed pneumatic system
Where it applies

Where pneumatic systems work applies

The same compressed air principles run through the vessel, the workshop and the terminal, so the same disciplined approach applies wherever air moves a valve or drives a tool.

  • Pneumatically operated valves on cargo, ballast and bunkering systems
  • Engine starting air and control air systems
  • Quick-closing valves, dampers and remote valve actuators
  • Ship whistle, horn and control air lines
  • Instrument and automation control air supply
  • Workshop and terminal compressed air lines, tools and equipment

Questions

Frequently asked

Can you repair pneumatic systems on board while the vessel is working?
Yes. Most valve, actuator, piping and leak work is carried out on board without taking the vessel out of service. We isolate the affected line, repair or replace the faulty components and pressure-test the section before returning it to operation. Larger overhauls that need a bench are done in our workshop.
How do you find a leak when the system loses pressure but nothing is obvious?
We work the circuit in sections. The line is pressurised and isolated valve by valve so the loss can be narrowed down, then confirmed by pressure-hold and flow checks. This finds leaks at fittings, seals and actuators that are not visible or audible under normal running, rather than replacing parts on assumption.
Do you supply the valves, actuators and fittings, or do we provide them?
Either. We can work with parts you hold on board, or source valves, actuators, seal kits and air-preparation components through our spare parts supply. Where a like-for-like part is not available quickly, we advise on a suitable equivalent so the system can be returned to service without a long wait.
Is the air source part of your work as well?
It can be. If the compressor, dryer or air quality is contributing to the fault, we address it as part of the job or together with our compressor repair. Clean, dry air at the correct pressure is what keeps valves and actuators reliable, so we treat the supply and the system as one.
Can you install a new pneumatic line or rebuild an existing one?
Yes. We route and fit new compressed air piping, install valves and actuators, and connect control air to the equipment it serves. New and rebuilt lines are supported, cleaned and pressure-tested before commissioning, then set and function-tested so the system performs correctly from the first run.
