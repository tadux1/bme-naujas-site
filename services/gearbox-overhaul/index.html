Gearboxes and transmissions

Marine Gearbox Overhaul and Maintenance

Marine gearbox overhaul for propulsion and auxiliary drivelines — we strip, inspect, repair and reassemble marine reduction gearboxes, renewing bearings, seals and gears so the vessel keeps full power to the shaft, on board or in our workshop.

Overview

Marine gearboxes restored to smooth, reliable running

A gearbox rarely fails without warning. Rising oil temperature, a change in noise or vibration, or metal particles in the lubricant are the early signs that bearings, gears or shafts are wearing. Left unattended, a marine reduction gearbox can lose drive to the propeller or seize entirely, taking propulsion or deck machinery out of service at the worst moment.

We work through the whole unit rather than replacing the first worn part. The gearbox is stripped, cleaned and measured — bearing clearances, gear tooth contact and backlash, shaft straightness and housing condition. From those measurements we decide what to renew and what to reuse, so the transmission overhaul restores the original tolerances instead of masking a deeper fault.

On reassembly we set the correct clearances and preload, refit renewed bearings and seals, and run the gearbox under test before it returns to service. Parts come through our spare parts supply to original specification, and where the work ties into main engine repair or shaft alignment we handle the driveline as one.

Often delivered alongside marine engine repair, seized bolt removal, marine spare parts.

Scope of work

What gearbox maintenance and overhaul includes

  • Strip-down and inspection — full disassembly, cleaning, dimensional checks and wear assessment of gears, shafts and housings
  • Bearing and seal renewal — main and auxiliary bearings, oil seals and gaskets replaced to manufacturer specification
  • Gear and pinion repair — tooth contact inspection, backlash measurement and renewal of worn gear sets
  • Shaft and journal repair — straightness checks, journal inspection, machining or renewal where required
  • Shaft alignment — coupling and driveline alignment to the engine and shaft after reassembly
  • Lubrication and cooling service — oil pump, cooler and filtration checks, oil sampling and system flushing
  • Reassembly and testing — build to clearances, no-load and load testing, temperature and vibration checks
  • Clutch and coupling overhaul — clutch packs and flexible couplings on reversing and reduction gearboxes inspected and renewed
Where it applies

Where gearbox overhaul applies

The same reduction and transmission principles run from the engine room to deck machinery and shoreside plant, so gearbox overhaul applies across the vessel and the terminal:

  • Main propulsion reduction gearboxes
  • Reversing and clutch gearboxes
  • Deck machinery drives — winches, windlasses and cranes
  • Bow thruster and auxiliary drive gearboxes
  • Pump and compressor drive gearboxes
  • Industrial gearboxes — conveyors, mills and terminal handling equipment

Questions

Frequently asked

Can you overhaul a marine gearbox on board, or does it have to come ashore?
Routine inspection, bearing and seal work and smaller repairs are often carried out on board or dockside. A full strip-down of a main reduction gearbox — with dimensional checks, gear renewal and machining — is usually done in our workshop, where we control cleanliness and have the tooling. We assess each case and recommend the option that returns the vessel to service safely.
How do you decide between a targeted repair and a full overhaul?
We open and measure the gearbox first — bearing clearances, gear tooth contact and backlash, shaft straightness and oil condition. If wear is confined to bearings, seals or a single gear set, a targeted repair is enough. Widespread wear, contamination or damage to shafts and housings calls for a full overhaul. You receive the findings and a clear recommendation before we proceed.
Can you supply the bearings, seals and gears, or do we source them?
Either way. Through our spare parts supply we source bearings, seals, gaskets and gear sets to the original specification, or we fit parts you provide. Where a component is obsolete, we can arrange manufacture to the measured dimensions rather than hold up the overhaul.
Do you align the shaft and driveline after the overhaul?
Yes. A gearbox is only as good as its alignment, so we check and correct coupling and driveline alignment to the engine and shaft as part of the work. When the overhaul is combined with main engine repair or shaft work, we align the whole driveline together rather than in isolation.
Do you work worldwide, and can you respond to a breakdown?
Yes. We attend vessels on board, in the yard and dockside worldwide, and offer 24/7 emergency response for gearbox breakdowns. Our engineers travel with the tooling needed for inspection and repair; where a seized coupling or fastener is holding up disassembly, our seized bolt removal service clears it without damaging the surrounding parts.
