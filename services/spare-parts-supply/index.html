Marine Spare Parts Supply

Marine spare parts supply keeps a vessel moving — we source, verify and deliver the OEM and equivalent replacement parts for engines, gearboxes, pumps and deck machinery, to the shipyard, dockside or on board, wherever the vessel happens to be.

Request a quote → All services
Overview

The right marine spare parts, verified and delivered

A missing part can hold an entire vessel in port. When a component fails at sea or during a port call, the difficulty is rarely the repair itself — it is finding the correct part quickly, confirming that it fits, and getting it to the vessel before the schedule slips.

We work from the equipment maker, model and serial number rather than a guessed description, so the part that arrives is the part that fits. Where an original is discontinued or the lead time is too long, we identify equivalent replacement parts of matching specification and pressure, temperature or material rating, and we tell you plainly which route we are taking.

Our marine parts sourcing network covers main engines, auxiliary machinery, gearboxes, pumps and hydraulics, and connects directly to the repair work our engineers carry out. When a part supports a main engine repair or gearbox overhaul, we coordinate the supply and the labour together so nothing waits on the quayside.

Often delivered alongside marine engine repair, marine gearbox overhaul, marine pump repair.

Scope of work

What spare parts supply includes

  • Part identification from equipment maker, model, serial number and nameplate data
  • OEM spare parts procurement direct from original manufacturers and authorised channels
  • Equivalent replacement parts sourced to matching specification when originals are discontinued or delayed
  • Engine and machinery spare parts — pistons, liners, bearings, seals, filters, gaskets and fasteners
  • Cross-referencing of part numbers and technical drawings to confirm fit before dispatch
  • Consolidation of multi-supplier orders into a single verified shipment
  • Spare parts delivery worldwide — to the shipyard, agent, warehouse or vessel in port
  • Documentation, packing lists and customs paperwork for international shipment
Where it applies

Where spare parts supply applies

The same sourcing discipline runs across the engine room, the deck and machinery ashore — the equipment differs, the need for the correct verified part does not.

  • Main engines and auxiliary generator sets
  • Gearboxes, shafting and propulsion systems
  • Pumps, compressors and heat exchangers
  • Deck machinery, winches and cranes
  • Hydraulic and pneumatic systems
  • Terminal and industrial machinery ashore

Questions

Frequently asked

Can you supply parts when the original is no longer manufactured?
Yes. When an OEM part is discontinued or the lead time is too long, we identify equivalent replacement parts that match the original specification — dimensions, pressure and temperature rating and material — and confirm the substitution with you before we order. Where a like-for-like original is essential, we source it through authorised channels instead.
How do you make sure the part will fit?
We work from the equipment maker, model and serial number and cross-reference part numbers against technical drawings and nameplate data, rather than a description alone. This confirms the correct revision and fit before anything is dispatched, which avoids the delay of a wrong part arriving at the vessel.
Do you deliver directly to the vessel?
Yes. We arrange spare parts delivery worldwide — to the shipyard, local agent, warehouse or the vessel in port — and consolidate multi-supplier orders into a single shipment with the packing lists and customs paperwork needed to clear it. For urgent breakdowns this runs alongside our 24/7 emergency response.
Can you both supply the parts and carry out the repair?
Often, yes. Spare parts supply connects directly to the work our engineers do, so a part supporting a pump repair, gearbox overhaul or main engine repair can be sourced and fitted as one coordinated job. Supplying and installing together removes the handover gap where a part waits on the quayside for labour, or the reverse.
